Bhimtal Lake is the largest natural lake in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand, located 22 km from Nainital at an altitude of 1,370 meters. Known for its scenic beauty, it features a central island with an aquarium, a Victorian-era dam from 1883, and offers boating, making it a quieter alternative to Nainital.

Nainital, known as the "Lake District" and "Jewel of Kumaon," is a popular hill station in Uttarakhand, India, located at an elevation of roughly 2,000 meters. Famous for the scenic, emerald-green Naini Lake, this former British summer retreat is surrounded by seven hills (Sapta-Shring) and offers boating, colonial architecture, and views of the snow-capped Himalayas.

Nainital is located in the Kumaon foothills of the outer Himalayas at a distance of 276 km (171 mi) from the state capital Dehradun and 314 km (195 mi) from New Delhi, the capital of India. Situated at an altitude of 1,938 metres (6,358 ft) above sea level, the town is set in a valley containing an eye-shaped lake, approximately two miles in circumference, and surrounded by mountains, of which the highest are Naina Peak (2,615 m (8,579 ft)) on the north, Deopatha (2,438 m (7,999 ft)) on the west, and Ayarpatha (2,278 m (7,474 ft)) on the south.
